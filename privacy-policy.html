SmartFind

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 16, 2026

SmartFind is designed with privacy as a core principle. The app operates entirely offline, stores all data locally on your device, and never connects to the internet.

1. Overview

SmartFind is an Android application that listens for incoming SMS messages matching a specific keyword exactly from designated phone numbers. The entire message must be only the keyword — no extra words or punctuation. When triggered, the app sounds a loud alarm to help you locate your misplaced device. This privacy policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected.

2. Data We Collect

SmartFind does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. There are no analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising SDKs, and no cloud services of any kind.

2.1 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored exclusively on your device in encrypted local storage (EncryptedSharedPreferences backed by Android's hardware keystore) and a local SQLite database:

Data Purpose Storage
Designated phone numbers To identify which contacts can trigger the alarm Encrypted preferences
Contact display names To show friendly names next to phone numbers in the app Encrypted preferences
Trigger keyword To identify the SMS content that triggers the alarm Encrypted preferences
Service enabled/disabled state To remember whether monitoring is active Encrypted preferences
Settings (cooldown period) To configure alarm trigger behavior Encrypted preferences
Last SMS check timestamp To avoid processing the same message twice Encrypted preferences
Audio volume/ringer state To restore your original settings after the alarm stops Encrypted preferences
Rate-limit timestamps To enforce a maximum of 5 alarm triggers per hour Encrypted preferences
Audit log events To maintain a tamper-evident record of alarm triggers, settings changes, and authentication attempts (phone numbers are redacted to last 4 digits; keywords are never logged) Local SQLite database

2.2 Data the App Does NOT Store

  • SMS message contents (messages are checked in memory and immediately discarded)
  • Full phone numbers in the audit log (only the last 4 digits are recorded)
  • Location data of any kind
  • Call logs
  • Your contacts database (contact names are only stored for numbers you explicitly add to SmartFind)
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or hardware information
  • Usage statistics or behavioral data

3. Permissions

SmartFind requires the following Android permissions to function. Each is used strictly for its stated purpose:

Permission Why It's Needed
RECEIVE_SMS To detect incoming trigger messages in real time
READ_PHONE_STATE To detect active phone calls (alarm is suppressed during calls) and to validate SMS subscription IDs for spoof detection
READ_CONTACTS To let you pick designated contacts from your address book when adding phone numbers
FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK To play the alarm sound reliably via a foreground service
WAKE_LOCK To keep the device awake while the alarm is playing
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED To restart SMS monitoring after the device reboots
ACCESS_NOTIFICATION_POLICY To override Do Not Disturb mode so the alarm is audible
USE_FULL_SCREEN_INTENT To display the alarm stop screen over the lock screen when triggered
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM To schedule the periodic contact reconfirmation reminder (every 6 months)
POST_NOTIFICATIONS Required on Android 13+ to show the persistent monitoring notification and alarm notifications

4. Internet Usage

SmartFind makes zero internet connections. The app does not require, request, or use the INTERNET permission. No data is ever transmitted over the network to any server. A network security configuration explicitly disables all cleartext traffic as a defense-in-depth measure.

No data leaves your device over the internet. SmartFind only reads incoming SMS messages through the standard Android SMS APIs and never sends any messages or network requests.

5. Location Data

SmartFind does not access, collect, or transmit any location data. The app does not use GPS, network location, or cell tower information. No location permissions are requested.

6. SMS Data

SmartFind reads incoming SMS messages solely to check whether they match your configured trigger conditions (designated number + keyword). Messages are processed in memory and are never stored or forwarded. Non-matching messages are ignored and discarded immediately.

When a trigger match occurs, the audit log records the event type (e.g., "alarm triggered") and the sender's phone number redacted to the last 4 digits. The message content is never logged.

SmartFind does not intercept, block, or hide any SMS messages. Incoming messages remain visible in your normal SMS app.

7. Audit Log

SmartFind maintains a local audit log of security-relevant events in a SQLite database on your device. This log records:

  • Alarm triggers, activations, and stops
  • Settings changes (e.g., service enabled/disabled, keyword changed, cooldown adjusted)
  • Authentication successes and failures
  • Rate-limit events and suppressed triggers (Android Auto, phone call, reception grace window)
  • SMS spoofing detection events

Phone numbers are redacted to the last 4 digits before being written to the audit log. Keywords are never logged. The log retains the most recent 500 events and is never transmitted off-device.

8. Authentication and Safety

SmartFind requires biometric authentication or your device PIN/pattern/password to change any configuration (enabling/disabling the service, changing the keyword, adding or removing contacts, adjusting settings). This protects against unauthorized changes.

The following safety features are permanently enabled and cannot be disabled:

  • Active use protection — the alarm is suppressed when you are actively using your phone (screen on and unlocked)
  • Android Auto protection — the alarm is suppressed while driving, replaced with a silent notification
  • Phone call protection — the alarm is suppressed during active phone calls
  • Lock screen privacy — notification details are hidden on the lock screen
  • Low battery protection — reduced alarm behavior at low battery to conserve power
  • Rate limiting — a maximum of 5 alarm triggers per hour
  • Cooldown period — a minimum 2-minute wait between alarms (configurable up to 30 minutes)

9. Device Administrator (Optional)

During first-run setup, SmartFind optionally offers to register as a device administrator. This feature only prevents the app from being silently uninstalled — it does not grant SmartFind any device management capabilities (no password enforcement, no remote wipe, no camera control, no encryption policy). You can disable this at any time through Settings > Security > Device Administrators.

10. Third-Party Libraries

SmartFind uses the following third-party libraries, none of which collect or transmit user data:

  • AndroidX WorkManager — schedules periodic SMS checks. Runs entirely on-device.
  • AndroidX Biometric — provides the authentication prompt. No data leaves the device.
  • AndroidX Security (EncryptedSharedPreferences) — encrypts stored settings using the hardware keystore. No data leaves the device.
  • AndroidX Room — local SQLite database for the audit log. No data leaves the device.
  • AndroidX AppCompat / Material Components — UI libraries. No data collection.

11. Data Sharing

SmartFind does not share any data with any third parties. There are no:

  • Analytics or tracking services
  • Advertising networks
  • Crash reporting tools
  • Cloud storage or backup services (cloud backup and device-to-device transfer are explicitly disabled)
  • Social media integrations

12. Data Retention and Deletion

All app data is stored locally on your device in encrypted preferences and a local SQLite database. The audit log automatically prunes old entries beyond the most recent 500 events. You can delete all data at any time by:

  • Going to Settings > Apps > SmartFind > Storage > Clear Data
  • Uninstalling the app (all data is permanently removed)

If device administrator is enabled, you must first disable it in Settings > Security > Device Administrators before uninstalling.

There is no server-side data to delete because no data is ever transmitted to any server. Cloud backup and device-to-device transfer are explicitly disabled, so no app data leaves your device through Android's backup system.

13. Security

SmartFind protects your data through multiple layers:

  • Encrypted storage — all settings (phone numbers, keyword, preferences) are encrypted using AES-256-GCM with keys stored in Android's hardware-backed keystore
  • Authentication — biometric or device PIN/pattern/password is required for all configuration changes
  • Sandboxed storage — app data is stored in Android's private app directory, inaccessible to other apps on non-rooted devices
  • Backup exclusion — cloud backup and device transfer are disabled to prevent data from leaving the device
  • No cleartext traffic — network cleartext traffic is explicitly disabled
  • SMS spoof detection — incoming trigger messages are validated against subscription IDs and service center addresses to detect spoofing attempts
  • Audit trail — all security-relevant events are logged with redacted PII for forensic accountability

14. Children's Privacy

SmartFind does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The app stores only the configuration data you explicitly enter (phone numbers, contact names, and trigger keyword).
